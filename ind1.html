Sinhgad Fort

Sinhagad Fort is a fort that was built 2000 years ago, and references to this fort can be found in the carvings of the Kaundinyeshwar temple. This fort has a long lost history associated with it, especially in terms of battles, and one such battle is the Battle of Sinhagad. It is built strategically with steep surrounding all around and has only two entrances to the fort, the Kalyan Darwaza and Pune Darwaza, this particular trail takes you through Pune Darwaza.


This fort has some of the most beautiful landscapes on the isolated cliff of the Sahyadri Mountains. Lush green environments and the mounts of western ghats are just amazing, then be it whatever the season is. There are a few challenging sections during this trail with rocks and all but nothing that cannot be crossed.


This trail begins from Sinhagad Paytha Road by the Askarwadi (Sinhgad Payatha) bus stop. Apart from that, this region is pretty well connected by roads and can be reached by taxi, bus, cab, or private vehicles. Even though the fort remains open throughout the year, but best months to visit this location is from June to February. There is a small fee to enter the fort, and the timings during which this fort operates is from 6 in the morning till 6 in the evening.

Kalsubai Peak Trail

This beautiful trail in the Shayadri takes one to the highest point, Kalsubai Peak. Verdant green environments make up the surroundings of this location that are not only green but highly biodiverse as well, with many different species of flora, fauna, and avifauna to explore and observe. Along with that comes the fantastic views that are on offer.


One can camp at night in this area, and it is just fabulous, with each season providing a different experience, monsoons and winters in particular. Another of the key highlights of this trail is the iron ladders, which are pretty narrow, and being the only path up and down, they can become a little crowded at times. Along with that, these ladders are ages old and have pretty significant gaps in them, not only that, they creek with each step, questioning the integrity of the structure. It's pretty much an adventure in itself.


Bari village, which is the starting point, can be reached using various different modes of transport such as buses, shared taxis, cabs, or even private vehicles. This is an amazing trail that provides thrill and adventure with some of the most magical views, making it one of the must-visit locations in this region.

Rajgad Fort Trek

Rajgarh directly translates to "Ruling Fort" in English, and it was the ruling fort of the Maratha Empire for almost 24 years. Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, one of the greatest Maratha Kings, made this his main ruling seat until he switched it to Raigarh. It has a long history and numerous stories, myths, and legends associated with it.


Apart from the great history, this location is known for its beautiful green environments that are not only green but highly biodiverse as well, with many different species of animals, birds, and plants to explore and observe.


It is one of the top tourist destinations of this location, and tourists in huge number visit almost on a daily basis which can maybe, at times, make it a little crowded. To avoid the crowd, one can opt to go on a weekday, avoid the swarm of tourists, and enjoy the whole trail in peace.


Trailhead for this particular trail lies by the Rajgarh Trek Starting Point, which can be reached easily by roads with various different modes of transport such as buses, taxis, cabs, tuk-tuks, or even private vehicles. It is highly recommended to avoid monsoons as the conditions becomes risky and slippery.

Vetal Tekdi Hike

Vetal Tekdi is a temple on the top of the highest hill in Pune. This hill gets its name from the temple hence the name Vetala Hill. It is a decent trek with views of Pune from the top. The vegetation on this hill is pretty decent. This is an amazing trail for people who wants a getaway from their busy everyday schedule. It also has amazing sunrise and sunset points.


This trail begins from the Vetal Tekdi Parking Lot, which can be reached using a taxi, cab, or private vehicle. The closest bus stops to this location are the Bhandarkar Road and Law College Road HDFC bus stops. One might find muddy conditions in this region during the monsoons, although the beauty increases exponentially.

Korigad Fort

Korigad Fort was built in the 15th century. It was built by Chatrapati Shivaji one of the greatest Maratha kings of all time. There are three temples on the top of the hill which looks sensational.


There is a 600-step climb to reach this location, although this particular trail takes one of the lesser-known alternate routes which meets up in the middle with the regular path. This place serves as an insight into the life of Chhatrapati Shivaji. There are many architectural insights into the history associated with it as well, differences in structure, placement of different rooms, etc., These things are just amazing to see and they even tell you about the different cultural beliefs. As the fort is very old a lot of stories accompany it, visit this fort to learn about the different cultural takes and to admire the beautiful views accompanied by it. Trailhead for this particular trail begins from Korigad Fort Parking which can be reached using a taxi, cab, or private vehicle.
